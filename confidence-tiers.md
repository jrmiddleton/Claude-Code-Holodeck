# Confidence Tier Methodology

## Why Tiers?

Not all historical figures left the same quality or quantity of primary sources. When simulating a conversation with a historical figure, the authenticity of that simulation depends directly on how much we know about how they actually thought, spoke, and expressed themselves.

We classify figures into confidence tiers to:

1. **Set expectations** -- a Tier 1 figure can be voiced with strong fidelity to their documented personality, vocabulary, and views. A Tier 2 figure requires more interpolation.
2. **Guide prompt design** -- higher-confidence figures get more specific behavioral constraints drawn from primary sources. Lower-confidence figures rely more on scholarly interpretation.
3. **Maintain intellectual honesty** -- rather than pretending all simulations are equally grounded, we acknowledge the spectrum of available evidence.

## Tier 1 -- High Confidence

Extensive primary sources exist: books they wrote, letters in their own hand, documented speeches, interviews, trial transcripts, or autobiographical writing. We can reconstruct not just *what* they thought, but *how* they expressed it -- their rhetorical style, humor, contradictions, and habits of mind.

| Figure | Key Primary Sources |
|---|---|
| **Leonardo da Vinci** | Notebooks (Codex Atlanticus, Codex Leicester, etc.), letters, treatises on painting |
| **Benjamin Franklin** | Autobiography, Poor Richard's Almanack, extensive correspondence, political writings |
| **Mark Twain** | Novels, essays, speeches, interviews, voluminous letters, autobiography |
| **Oscar Wilde** | Plays, essays, fairy tales, letters (especially *De Profundis*), trial transcripts |
| **Albert Einstein** | Scientific papers, personal letters (to Besso, Born, etc.), interviews, autobiographical notes |
| **Richard Feynman** | Lectures (*Feynman Lectures on Physics*), popular books, interviews, letters, Los Alamos anecdotes |
| **Marcus Aurelius** | *Meditations* -- direct philosophical journaling, imperial correspondence |
| **Voltaire** | Extensive published works (novels, essays, plays), thousands of surviving letters |
| **Jordan Peterson** | Published books, thousands of hours of podcast/lecture recordings, interviews |
| **Joe Rogan** | Thousands of podcast episodes, comedy specials, UFC commentary, interviews |
| **Lex Fridman** | Hundreds of podcast episodes, MIT lectures, research papers, interviews |
| **Thomas Jefferson** | 19,000+ surviving letters, *Notes on the State of Virginia*, autobiography, political writings |
| **Alexander Hamilton** | Federalist Papers (51 of 85), Treasury reports, extensive correspondence, legal briefs |
| **Steve Jobs** | Extensive interviews, keynote presentations, authorized Isaacson biography, internal emails |
| **Winston Churchill** | *The Second World War* (6 vols.), speeches, letters, memos, paintings, Nobel Prize literature |
| **Napoleon Bonaparte** | 33,000+ letters, St. Helena memoirs, military dispatches, the Napoleonic Code |
| **Leonard Cohen** | Poetry collections, novels, song lyrics, extensive filmed interviews, letters |
| **David Bowie** | Hundreds of interviews, song lyrics, essays, documentary footage, art exhibitions |
| **Friedrich Nietzsche** | Extensive published philosophy, autobiography (*Ecce Homo*), letters |
| **Albert Camus** | Novels, essays, journalism, notebooks, letters, Nobel acceptance speech |
| **Simone de Beauvoir** | *The Second Sex*, four volumes of memoirs, letters, filmed interviews |

## Tier 2 -- Good Confidence

Substantial historical records exist, but the figure's direct voice is less fully preserved. Sources may be filtered through translation, attribution is sometimes uncertain, or the figure is known primarily through others' accounts.

| Figure | Key Primary Sources | Caveats |
|---|---|---|
| **Galileo** | Scientific writings, letters, Inquisition trial records | Some works heavily edited for publication |
| **Michelangelo** | Letters, poetry, documented conversations (Condivi, Vasari) | Biographers shaped the narrative |
| **Marie Curie** | Scientific papers, letters, brief autobiography | Reserved personality limits personal voice in writing |
| **Adam Smith** | *The Wealth of Nations*, *The Theory of Moral Sentiments*, lecture notes | Requested most personal papers be burned |
| **Jane Austen** | Novels, surviving letters | Many letters destroyed by her sister Cassandra |
| **Sun Tzu** | *The Art of War* (attributed) | Authorship debated; may be composite work |
| **Socrates** | Documented through Plato, Xenophon, Aristophanes | Wrote nothing himself; all sources are secondhand |
| **Nikola Tesla** | Autobiography (*My Inventions*), patents, lectures, interviews | Later-life claims increasingly unverifiable |
| **Ada Lovelace** | Notes on the Analytical Engine, letters to Babbage, personal correspondence | Limited surviving material; scholarly debate on contributions |
| **Freddie Mercury** | Interviews, song lyrics, band member accounts, biographies | Notably private; much known through others' accounts |
